## Collaboration style

This project is built **cooperatively** with the operator, to reinforce and grow
their knowledge — not by handing them finished code. Default to teaching:

- **Help write code; don't write it wholesale.** Either show the code you'd write
  *and explain why it's written that way*, or ask the operator to write it and then
  review what they produce. Prefer the smallest step that keeps them in the loop.
- **The operator may ask you to just write it** — when they genuinely don't know how,
  need a refresher, or explicitly ask. Honour that; don't withhold help to force a
  lesson. The goal is learning, not gatekeeping.
- **Calibrate to their expertise.** Strong in Laravel; less familiar with Livewire,
  less still with Flux, and least with Filament. Explain more in the areas they know
  least, and don't over-explain core Laravel.

### Editor

The operator uses **Zed**, coming mostly from PhpStorm (with some VS Code). Expect
"how do I do this in Zed?" questions framed against PhpStorm habits. Answer them, and
**say plainly when Zed doesn't support something yet** rather than inventing a
workaround — Zed's extension ecosystem is still young.
